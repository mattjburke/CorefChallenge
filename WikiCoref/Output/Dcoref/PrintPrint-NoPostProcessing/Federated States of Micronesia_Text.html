(1)The Federated States of Micronesia -LRB- FSM -RRB- is an independent sovereign island nation consisting of four states -- from west to east , Yap , Chuuk , Pohnpei and Kosrae -- that are spread across the Western Pacific Ocean .
(2) Together , the states comprise around 607 islands -LRB- a combined land area of approximately 702 km2 -RRB- that cover a longitudinal distance of almost just north of the equator .
(3) They lie northeast of New Guinea , south of Guam and the Marianas , west of Nauru and the Marshall Islands , east of Palau and the Philippines , about north of eastern Australia and some southwest of the main islands of Hawaii .
(4) While the FSM 's total land area is quite small , it occupies more than 2600000 km2 of the Pacific Ocean .
(5) The capital is Palikir , located on Pohnpei Island , while the largest city is Weno , located in the Chuuk Atoll .
(6) Each of its four states is centered around one or more main high islands , and all but Kosrae include numerous outlying atolls .
(7) The Federated States of Micronesia is spread across part of the Caroline Islands in the wider region of Micronesia , which consists of thousands of small islands divided between several countries .
(8) The term Micronesia may refer to the Federated States or to the region as a whole .
(9) The FSM was formerly a part of the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands -LRB- TTPI -RRB- , a United Nations Trust Territory under U.S. administration , but it formed its own constitutional government on May 10 , 1979 , becoming a sovereign state after independence was attained on November 3 , 1986 under a Compact of Free Association with the United States .
(10) Other neighboring island entities , and also former members of the TTPI , formulated their own constitutional governments and became the Republic of the Marshall Islands -LRB- RMI -RRB- and the Republic of Palau -LRB- ROP -RRB- .
(11) The FSM has a seat in the United Nations .
(12) The ancestors of the Micronesians settled over four thousand years ago .
(13) A decentralized chieftain-based system eventually evolved into a more centralized economic and religious empire centered on Yap .
(14) Nan Madol , consisting of a series of small artificial islands linked by a network of canals , is often called the Venice of the Pacific .
(15) It is located on the eastern periphery of the island of Pohnpei and used to be the ceremonial and political seat of the Saudeleur dynasty that united Pohnpei 's estimated 25,000 people from about AD 500 until 1500 , when the centralized system collapsed .
(16) European explorers -- first the Portuguese in search of the Spice Islands -LRB- Indonesia -RRB- and then the Spanish -- reached the Carolines in the sixteenth century .
(17) The Spanish incorporated the archipelago to the Spanish East Indies and in the 19th century established a number of outposts and missions .
(18) In 1887 they founded the town of Santiago de la Ascension in what today is Kolonia on the island of Pohnpei .
(19) Following the Spanish-American War the Spanish sold the archipelago to Germany in 1899 .
(20) Later it was conquered by Japan in 1914 , and later by the United States during World War II and administered by the U.S. under United Nations auspices in 1947 as part of the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands .
(21) During World War II , a significant portion of the Japanese fleet was based in Truk Lagoon .
(22) In February 1944 , Operation Hailstone , one of the most important naval battles of the war , took place at Truk , in which many Japanese support vessels and aircraft were destroyed .
(23) On May 10 , 1979 , four of the Trust Territory districts ratified a new constitution to become the Federated States of Micronesia .
(24) Palau , the Marshall Islands , and the Northern Mariana Islands chose not to participate .
(25) The FSM signed a Compact of Free Association with the United States of America , which entered into force on November 3 , 1986 , marking Micronesia 's emergence from trusteeship to independence .
(26) The Compact was renewed in 2004 .
(27) The Federated States of Micronesia is governed by the 1979 constitution , which guarantees fundamental human rights and establishes a separation of governmental powers .
(28) The unicameral Congress has fourteen members elected by popular vote .
(29) Four senators -- one from each state -- serve four-year terms ; the remaining ten senators represent single-member districts based on population , and serve two-year terms .
(30) The President and Vice President are elected by Congress from among the four state-based senators to serve four-year terms in the executive branch .
(31) Their congressional seats are then filled by special elections .
(32) The president and vice president are supported by an appointed cabinet .
(33) There are no formal political parties .
(34) In international politics , the Federated States of Micronesia has often voted with the United States with respect to United Nations General Assembly resolutions .
(35) These states are further divided into municipalities .
(36) The Federated States of Micronesia consists of 607 islands extending across the archipelago of the Caroline Islands east of the Philippines .
(37) The islands are grouped into four states , which are Yap , Chuuk -LRB- called Truk until January 1990 -RRB- , Pohnpei -LRB- known as `` Ponape '' until November 1984 -RRB- , and Kosrae -LRB- formerly Kusaie -RRB- .
(38) These four states are each represented by a white star on the national flag .
(39) The capital is Palikir , on Pohnpei .
(40) Economic activity in the Federated States of Micronesia consists primarily of subsistence farming and fishing .
(41) The islands have few mineral deposits worth exploiting , except for high-grade phosphate .
(42) Long line tuna fishing is also viable with foreign vessels from China operated in the 1990s .
(43) The potential for a tourist industry exists , but the remoteness of the location and a lack of adequate facilities hinder development .
(44) Financial assistance from the U.S. is the primary source of revenue , with the U.S. pledged to spend $ 1.3 billion in the islands in 1986 -- 2001 .
(45) Geographical isolation and a poorly developed infrastructure are major impediments to long-term growth .
(46) The nation uses the U.S. dollar as its currency .
(47) The Federated States of Micronesia is served by four international airports .
(48) The indigenous population of the Federated States of Micronesia , which is predominantly Micronesian , consists of various ethnolinguistic groups .
(49) It has a nearly 100 % Pacific Islander and Asian population .
(50) Chuukese 48.8 % , Pohnpeian 24.2 % , Kosraean 6.2 % , Yapese 5.2 % , Yap outer islands 4.5 % , Asian 1.8 % , Polynesian 1.5 % , other 6.4 % , unknown 1.4 % .
(51) A sizeable minority also have some Japanese ancestry , which is a result of intermarriages between Japanese settlers and Micronesians during the Japanese colonial period .
(52) There is also growing expatriate population of Americans , Australians , Europeans , and residents from China and the Philippines since the 1990s .
(53) English has become the common language of the government , and for secondary and tertiary education .
(54) Outside of the main capital towns of the four FSM states , the local languages are primarily spoken .
(55) Population growth remains high at more than 3 % annually , offset somewhat by net emigration .
(56) Pohnpei is notable for the prevalence of the extreme form of color blindness known as maskun .
(57) Each of the four States has its own culture and traditions , but there are also common cultural and economic bonds that are centuries old .
(58) For example , cultural similarities like the importance of the traditional extended family and clan systems can be found on all the islands .

(64) The languages of the Federated States of Micronesia include English -LRB- the official and common language -RRB- , and the country has seven official languages : English , Pohnpeian , Woleaian , Yapese , Kosraean , and Chuukese -LRB- Trukese -RRB- .
(65) Other languages spoken in the country include Pingelapese , Ngatikese , Satawalese , Puluwatese , Mortlockese , and Mokilese .

(67) There have been few published literary writers from the Federated States of Micronesia .

(69) Several Protestant denominations , as well as the Roman Catholic Church , are present in every Micronesian state .
(70) Most Protestant groups trace their roots to American Congregationalist missionaries .
(71) On the island of Kosrae , the population is approximately 7,800 ; 95 percent are Protestants .
(72) On Pohnpei , the population of 35,000 is evenly divided between Protestants and Catholics .
(73) On Chuuk and Yap , an estimated 60 percent are Catholic and 40 percent are Protestant .
(74) Religious groups with small followings include Baptists , Assemblies of God , Salvation Army , Seventh-day Adventists , Jehovah 's Witnesses , the Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints -LRB- Mormons -RRB- , and the Baha'i Faith .

(80) More Protestants live on the western side of the island , while more Catholics live on the eastern side .
(81) Missionaries of many religious traditions are present and operate freely .
(82) The Constitution provides for freedom of religion , and the Government generally respected this right in practice .
(83) The US government received no reports of societal abuses or discrimination based on religious belief or practice in 2007 .
(84) The FSM is a sovereign , self-governing state in free association with the United States , which is wholly responsible for its defense .
(85) The Division of Maritime Surveillance operates a paramilitary Maritime Wing and a small Maritime Police Unit .
(86) The Compact of Free Association allows FSM citizens to join the U.S. military without having to obtain U.S. permanent residency or citizenship , allows for immigration and employment for Micronesians in the U.S. , and establishes economic and technical aid programs .
