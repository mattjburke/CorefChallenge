(19) Aberfoyle is also part of the Loch Lomond and The Trossachs National Park .
(20) Aberfoyle is also home to the largest Go Ape adventure course in the UK , featuring the longest zip-line in the UK .
(21) Aberfoyle has connections to many historical figures such as Rob Roy and Mary , Queen of Scots .
(22) Robert Roy MacGregor was born at the head of nearby Loch Katrine , and his well known cattle stealing exploits took him all around the area surrounding Aberfoyle .
(23) There currently stands a tree in the village that MacGregor was reputed to have climbed and hid in to escape the clutches of the law .
(24) Also , Mary , Queen of Scots , visited nearby Inchmahome Priory often as a child , and during her short reign .
(25) She also used the priory during her short reign , particularly in 1547 , where she felt safe from the English Army .
(26) However , the most local historical figure is the Reverend Robert Kirk , born in 1644 .
(27) It was the Rev. Kirk who provided the first translation into Gaidhlig of the Book of Psalms , however , he is better remembered for the publication of his book `` The Secret Commonwealth of Elves , Fauns , and Fairies '' in 1691 .
(28) Kirk had long been researching fairies , and the book collected several personal accounts and stories of folk who claimed to have encountered them .
(29) It was after this , while Kirk was minister of Aberfoyle parish , that he died in unusual circumstances .
(30) Kirk had long believed that the local Doon Hill , was the gateway to the `` Secret Commonwealth '' , or the land of the Fairies .
(31) It was a place that Kirk visited often , taking daily walks there from his manse .
(32) The story goes that the Fairies of Doon Hill were angry with the Rev. Kirk for going into the domain of the Unseelie court , where he had been warned not to go , and decided to imprison him in Doon Hill -- for one night in May 1692 , the Rev. Kirk went out for a walk to the hill , in his nightshirt .
(33) Some accounts claim that he simply vanished , however he suddenly collapsed .
(34) He was found and brought home , but died soon afterwards .
(35) He was buried in his own kirkyard , although local legends claim that the fairies took his body away , and the coffin contains only stones .
(36) The huge pine tree that still stands at the top of Doon Hill is said to contain Kirk 's imprisoned spirit .
(37) Kirk 's cousin , Graham of Duchray , was then to claim that the spectre of Kirk had visited him in the night , and told him that he had been carried off by the Fairies .
(38) Having left his widow expecting a child , the spectre of Kirk told Graham that he would appear at the baptism , whereupon Graham was to throw an iron knife at the apparition , thus freeing Kirk from the Fairies ' clutches .
(39) However , when Kirk 's spectre appeared , Graham was apparently too shocked by the vision to throw the knife , and Kirk 's ghost faded away forever .
(40) Today , visitors to Doon Hill write their wishes on pieces of white silk , or other white cloth , and tie them to the branches of the trees for the Fairies to grant .
(41) It is also said that if one runs around the great pine tree at the summit seven times , then the Fairies will appear .
(42) In recent years the largest of the trees , Kirk 's `` Minister 's Pine '' , has been damaged by vandals who have hammered coins into the bark .
(43) Other Scots pines in the surrounding area are increasingly commonly festooned with tied bits of plastic , crisp packets and the like rather than ` white pieces of silk ' .
