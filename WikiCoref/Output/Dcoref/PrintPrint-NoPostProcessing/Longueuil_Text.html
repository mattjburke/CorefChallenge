(1)Longueuil is a city in the province of Quebec , Canada .
(2) It is the seat of the Montérégie administrative region and the central city of the Urban agglomeration of Longueuil .
(3) It sits on the south shore of the Saint Lawrence River directly across from Montreal .
(4) The population as of the Canada 2011 Census totaled 231,409 , making it Montreal 's second largest suburb , the fifth most populous city in Quebec and nineteenth largest in Canada .
(5) Charles Le Moyne founded Longueuil as a seigneurie in 1657 .
(6) It would become a parish in 1845 , a village in 1848 , a town in 1874 and a city in 1920 .
(7) Between 1961 and 2002 , Longueuil 's borders grew three times , as it was amalgamated with surrounding municipalities ; there was a partial de-amalgamation in 2006 -LRB- see 2000 -- 2006 municipal reorganization in Quebec -RRB- .
(8) Longueuil is a residential , commercial and industrial city .
(9) It incorporates some urban features , but is essentially a suburb .
(10) Longueuil can be classified as a commuter town as a large portion of its residents commute to work in Montreal .
(11) Most buildings are single-family homes constructed in the post-war period .
(12) The city consists of three boroughs : Le Vieux-Longueuil , Saint-Hubert and Greenfield Park .
(13) Longueuil is the seat of the judicial district of Longueuil .
(14) Residents of the city are called Longueuillois .
(15) The territory of New France was divided into seigneuries in order to ensure the colony 's defence .
(16) Longueuil was founded in 1657 by Charles Le Moyne , a merchant from Ville-Marie -LRB- present day Montreal -RRB- , as a seigneurie .
(17) According to Abbé Faillon , Charles Le Moyne , lord of the area starting in 1657 , named Longueuil after a village which is today the seat of a canton in the district of Dieppe in his homeland of Normandy .
(18) In France , the name is spelled `` Longueil '' and it is rumored that it was a mistake to spell it `` Longueuil '' .
(19) His son , Charles Le Moyne de Longueuil , built Fort Longueuil as his fortified residence .
(20) It was constructed of stone between 1685 and 1690 and had four towers .
(21) Fort Longueuil was believed to be occupied by American troops during the American Revolutionary War .
(22) It was subsequently occupied by the British .
(23) It was demolished in 1810 due to its poor condition .
(24) The archaeological remains of Fort Longueuil were recognized as a National Historic Site of Canada on May 25 , 1923 .
(25) The site extends beneath the present-day Saint-Antoine-de-Padoue Cathedral .
(26) The seigneurial system ended in 1845 and Longueuil was turned into a parish municipality named Saint-Antoine-de-Longueuil .
(27) In 1848 , a portion detached from the parish and officially established as the village of Longueuil .
(28) This same village became a town in 1874 , and then a city in 1920 .
(29) Musician Paul Pratt notably served as the city 's mayor from 1935-1966 .
(30) Longueuil 's city limits expanded for the first time in 1961 when it merged with Montréal-Sud , and again in 1969 when it merged with Ville Jacques-Cartier .
(31) In both cases , Longueuil was chosen as the name of the new city .
(32) On January 1 , 2002 , as part of the 2000 -- 2006 municipal reorganization in Quebec , the provincial government amalgamated the former Longueuil with Boucherville , Brossard , Greenfield Park , LeMoyne , Saint-Bruno-de-Montarville , Saint-Hubert and Saint-Lambert .
(33) As with the 1960s , the name Longueuil was chosen for the new city .
(34) However , after a change of government and a 2004 referendum , Boucherville , Brossard , Saint-Lambert and Saint-Bruno-de-Montarville were re-constituted as independent cities on January 1 , 2006 .
(35) As such , the current city of Longueuil now includes only the former cities of Longueuil -LRB- 1969-2002 -RRB- , Saint-Hubert , Greenfield Park and LeMoyne .
(36) Longueuil occupies 115.59 square kilometres -LRB- 44.6 sq mi -RRB- of land .
(37) The city is bordered by the cities of Saint-Lambert and Brossard to the west , Boucherville to the east , Saint-Bruno-de-Montarville to the south and the Saint Lawrence River and Montreal to the north .
(38) The city of Longueuil is located approximately 7 kilometres -LRB- 5 mi -RRB- south of Montreal on the south shore of the Saint Lawrence River .
(39) Longueuil is located in the Saint Lawrence River valley , and is a vast plain .
(40) Areas near the river were originally swamp land with mixed forest , and later prime agricultural land .
(41) Agricultural land still exists in the portions of the city furthest from the river .
(42) The city of Longueuil also includes Île Charron , a small island in the Saint Lawrence River , and part of the Boucherville Islands .
(43) Like Montreal , Longueuil is classified as humid continental or hemiboreal -LRB- Köppen climate classification Dfb -RRB- .
(44) Longueuil has long winters , lasting from November to March , short springs during April and May , average summers , lasting from June to August , and short autumns during September and October .
(45) According to the 2011 Canadian Census , the city of Longueuil had 231,409 people , an increase of 0.9 % over 2006 's figure of 229,330 .
(46) Longueuil occupies 115.59 square kilometres of space , giving the city a population density of 2,002 persons per kilometre squared .
(47) There were 106,499 private dwellings , 102,067 of which were occupied by usual residents .
(48) Of the 132,570 workers in Longueuil , the median income was $ 26,537 , which is above Quebec 's provincial average of $ 25,464 .
(49) Among the 69,990 full-time workers , the median income was $ 37,521 or slightly below the provincial average .
(50) As of the 2011 Canadian Census , French was the mother tongue language of 81.4 % of Longueuil 's residents while English was the first language of 7.5 % .
(51) Other languages were spoken by 13.6 % of the population , with the most spoken being Spanish -LRB- 3.0 % -RRB- , Arabic -LRB- 2.1 % -RRB- , Haitian Creole -LRB- 1.2 % -RRB- , Romanian -LRB- 0.7 % -RRB- , Persian -LRB- 0.7 % -RRB- , Chinese -LRB- 0.6 % -RRB- , Italian -LRB- 0.6 % -RRB- and Portuguese -LRB- 0.6 % -RRB- .
(52) These figures include multiple responses .
(53) People of European origins made up 87.6 % of the population in 2006 .
(54) The largest visible minority groups are Black -LRB- 4.1 % -RRB- , Latin American -LRB- 2 % -RRB- , Arab -LRB- 1.6 % -RRB- , Chinese -LRB- 1.2 % -RRB- , Southeast Asian -LRB- 1 % -RRB- , and South Asian -LRB- 0.7 % -RRB- .
(55) Although a large chunk of Longueuil 's work force commute to Montreal , the city nevertheless offers many jobs in a diverse range of industries .
(56) Above all , Longueuil benefits from having very low property value despite its close proximity to Montreal .
(57) Longueuil is particularly strong in the aerospace industry .
(58) It is home to the headquarters of both Pratt & Whitney Canada and Héroux-Devtek .
(59) Pratt & Whitney Canada is Longueuil 's top employer with 5,000 employees , while Héroux-Devtek has 550 employees .
(60) Also located in Longueuil is the headquarters of the Canadian Space Agency -LRB- John H. Chapman Space Center -RRB- , adjacent to Montréal / Saint-Hubert Airport .

(62) Other companies based in Longueuil include Agropur and Jean Coutu Group .
(63) In 2008 , Canadian Business ranked Longueuil as the 30th best place to do business in Canada .
(64) There are several notable cultural events in Longueuil each year .
(65) The Longueuil International Percussion Festival , which features 500 musicians , takes place over six days in July in the neighbourhood of Old Longueuil , and draws 200,000 visitors per year .
(66) There are three nature parks in Longueuil , Parc Marie-Victorin and Parc Michel-Chartrand in Le Vieux-Longueuil and Parc de la Cité in Saint-Hubert .
(67) There are seven arenas : Cynthia Coull Arena in Greenfield Park ; Aréna Émile-Butch-Bouchard , Aréna Jacques-Cartier , Aréna Olympia and Colisée Jean Béliveau in Le Vieux-Longueuil ; and Centre sportif Gaétan-Boucher and Centre sportif Rosanne-Laflamme in Saint-Hubert .

(93) The Saint Lawrence River between the Island of Montreal and the south shore is traversed by only five automobile crossings .
