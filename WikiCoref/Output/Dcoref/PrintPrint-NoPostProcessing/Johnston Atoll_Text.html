(1)Johnston Atoll is a 1.03 sqmi atoll in the North Pacific Ocean about 750 nmi west of Hawaii .
(2) The atoll , which is located on the coral reef platform , comprises four islands .
(3) Johnston and Sand islands are both enlarged natural features , while North -LRB- Akau -RRB- and East -LRB- Hikina -RRB- are two artificial islands formed by coral dredging .
(4) Johnston Atoll is grouped as one of the United States Minor Outlying Islands .
(5) For nearly 70 years , the atoll was under the control of the American military .
(6) In that time it was used as an airbase , a naval refuelling depot and a weapons testing area .
(7) In the mid-1980s , the atoll became a facility for chemical weapons disposal .
(8) In 2004 the military base was closed ; island control was handed over to civilian authorities .
(9) Johnston is an unincorporated territory of the United States administered by the United States Fish and Wildlife Service of the Department of the Interior as part of the Pacific Remote Islands Marine National Monument .
(10) The first Western record of the atoll was on September 2 , 1796 when the American brig Sally accidentally grounded on a shoal near the islands .
(11) The ship 's captain , Joseph Pierpont , published his experience in several American newspapers the following year giving an accurate position of Johnston and Sand Island along with part of the reef .
(12) However he did not name or lay claim to the area .
(13) The islands were not officially named until Captain Charles J. Johnston of the Royal Naval ship sighted them on December 14 , 1807 .
(14) In 1858 William Parker and R. F. Ryan , chartered the schooner Palestine specifically to find Johnston Atoll .
(15) They located guano on the atoll in March 1858 and they proceed to claim the island under the Guano Islands Act .
(16) By 1858 , Johnston Atoll was claimed by both the United States and the Kingdom of Hawaii .
(17) In June 1858 , Samuel Allen , sailing on the Kalama , tore down the U.S. flag and raised the Hawaiian flag .
(18) On July 27 , 1858 , the atoll was declared part of the domain of King Kamehameha IV .
(19) However , later that year King Kamehameha revoked the lease granted to Allen when the King learned that the atoll had been claimed previously by the United States .
(20) By 1890 the atoll 's entire guano deposits had been depleted -LRB- mined out -RRB- by U.S. interests operating under the Guano Islands Act .
(21) From July 10 to July 22 , 1923 , the atoll was recorded in a pioneering aerial photography project .
(22) In 1923 by Executive Order No. 4467 , President Calvin Coolidge established Johnston Atoll as a federal bird refuge and placed it under the control of the U.S. Department of Agriculture .
(23) On December 29 , 1934 , President Franklin D. Roosevelt transferred control of Johnston Atoll to the United States Navy in order to establish an air station , and also to the Department of the Interior to administer the bird refuge .
(24) In 1936 , the Navy began to develop a base for seaplanes and an airstrip with refueling facilities .
(25) On February 1941 Johnston Atoll was designated as a Naval Defensive Sea Area and Airspace Reservation .
(26) The atoll was briefly shelled by Japanese naval units shortly after the attack on Pearl Harbor in December 1941 .
(27) But as the Pacific War soon shifted west , the island 's role changed from being a combat outpost to a refueling and resupply base for American aircraft and submarines .
(28) On November 1 , 1957 , a United States Coast Guard LORAN-A station was commissioned -LRB- on Sand Island -RRB- which switched to a LORAN-C station in 1979 .
(29) The station was disestablished on July 1 , 1992 .
(30) The Johnston Atoll area was used during the 1950s and 1960s as an American nuclear weapons test site -- for both above-ground and underground nuclear tests .
(31) Later on , it became the site of a chemical weapons depot and the site of the Johnston Atoll Chemical Agent Disposal System -LRB- JACADS -RRB- .
(32) All of the chemical weapons that were once stored on Johnston Island have been incinerated , and that process was completed in 2000 and JACADS demolished by 2003 .
(33) Between 1958 and 1975 , several scientific sounding rockets were launched from Johnston Island .
(34) There were also several nuclear test missiles that were launched from Johnston Island in 1962 during the `` Operation Dominic '' series of nuclear tests , from a launchpad at .
(35) Twelve thermonuclear warheads were exploded in all , one of which was deliberately disrupted when the PGM-17 Thor carrying it failed to launch scattering plutonium debris over the island .
(36) Afterwards , the radioactive debris and soils were placed in a 25 acre landfill on the island , along with residue from Agent Orange containers returned from Southeast Asia after the Vietnam War , PCBs , PAHs , dioxins , and sarin nerve gas from the Soviet Union and East Germany .
(37) citation needed In 1963 , the U.S. Senate ratified the Limited Test Ban Treaty , which contained a provision known as `` Safeguard C '' .
(38) Safeguard C was the basis for maintaining Johnston Atoll as a `` ready to test '' above-ground nuclear testing site should atmospheric nuclear testing ever be deemed to be necessary again .
(39) In 1993 , Congress appropriated no funds for the Johnston Atoll `` Safeguard C '' mission , bringing it to an end .
(40) Congress redefined the island 's military mission as the storage and destruction of chemical weapons .
(41) It was used for rocket-launched nuclear tests in the 1950s .
(42) In the 1960s it was the site for the operational Program 437 anti-satellite system .
(43) Several sounding rockets were also launched over the years .
(44) Known to have been used for 124 launches from 1958 to 1975 , reaching up to 1158 kilometers altitude .
(45) In 2003 , all structures and facilities , including those used in JACADS , were removed , and the runway was marked closed .
(46) The last flight out for official personnel was June 15 , 2004 .
(47) After this date , the base was completely deserted , with the only structure left standing being the JOC building at the east end of the runway .
(48) On August 22 , 2006 , Johnston Island was struck by Hurricane Ioke .
(49) The eastern eye-wall passed directly over the atoll , with winds exceeding 100 mph .
(50) Twelve people were actually on the island when the hurricane struck , part of a contracted USAF crew sent to the island to monitor groundwater contamination levels .
(51) All 12 survived .
(52) On December 9 , 2007 , the United States Coast Guard swept the runway at Johnston Island of debris and used the runway in the removal and rescue of an ill Taiwanese fisherman to Oahu , Hawaii .
(53) The fisherman was transferred from the Taiwanese fishing vessel Sheng Yi Tsai No. 166 to the Coast Guard buoy tender Kukui on December 6 , 2007 .
(54) The fisherman was transported to the island , and then picked up by a Coast Guard HC-130 Hercules rescue plane from Kodiak , Alaska .
(55) Since the base was closed , the atoll is likely to have been visited by sailing vessels crossing the Pacific , as the deserted atoll has a strong lure due to the activities once performed there .
(56) One vessel blogged about stopping there for several days during a trip from Honolulu to the Marshall Islands .
(57) In 2010 , a Fish and Wildlife survey team identified a swarm of Anoplolepis ants that had invaded the island .
(58) The crazy ants are particularly destructive to the native wildlife , and needed to be eradicated .
(59) A `` Crazy Ant Strike Team '' was formed to stay on the island for nine months to bait traps for the ants and eliminate them .
(60) The team camped in the old chemical weapons storage bunkers on the southwest corner of the island .
(61) It is believed that the ants arrived with private boaters visiting the island illegally .
(62) Dr Lisa Lobel 's work at the Atoll on the impact of PCB contamination in reef damselfish -LRB- Abudefduf sordidus -RRB- demonstrated that embryonic abnormalities could be utilized as a metric for comparing contaminated and uncontaminated areas .
(63) In 1923 , Johnston Atoll was designated a federal bird refuge by President Calvin Coolidge with Executive Order 4467 .
(64) The island was later converted to a National Wildlife Refuge and now includes of land and over of water area .
(65) While the outer islets and water rights are part administered by the Fish and Wildlife Service , the actual land mass remains under Navy control for cleanup purposes .

(68) Johnston Atoll has never had any indigenous inhabitants , although during the late part of the 20th century , there were averages of about 300 American military personnel and 1,000 civilian contractors present at any given time .
(69) The central means of transportation to this island was the airport , which had a paved military runway .

(80) Due to the atoll 's tilt , much of the reef on the southeast portion has subsided .

(82) Its climate is tropical but generally dry .

(84) With elevation ranging from sea level to 5 m at Summit Peak , the islands contain some low-growing vegetation on mostly flat terrain and no natural fresh water resources .
