(1)Los Angeles Pierce College , also known as Pierce College , Pierce , is a community college that serves more than 23,000 students in the northern Chalk Hills of Woodland Hills , a community within the San Fernando Valley region of the city of Los Angeles , California .
(2) The college began with 70 students and 18 faculty members on September 15 , 1947 .
(3) Originally known as the Clarence W. Pierce School of Agriculture , the institution 's initial focus was crop cultivation and animal husbandry .
(4) Nine years later , in 1956 , the school was renamed to Los Angeles Pierce College , retaining the name of its founder , Dr. Pierce , as well as his commitment to agricultural and veterinary study .
(5) -LRB- Pierce still maintains a 225 acre working farm for hands-on training . -RRB-
(6) Pierce College offers courses on more than 100 subjects in 92 academic disciplines , and has transfer alliances with most of the universities in the state .
(7) Students at the school successfully transfer to UC and CSU schools .
(8) Students can pursue any of the 44 Associate 's degrees or 78 Certificates of Achievement the school offers directly .
(9) Pierce College comprises 426 acre amidst a dense metropolis , an area larger than many university campuses , including that of UCLA .
(10) The grounds are landscaped with more than 2,200 trees , thousands of roses and a 1.9 acre botanical garden .
(11) The Pierce College farm houses small herds of cattle , sheep , goats , pigs , a small poultry flock , as well as a llama and an alpaca for its students to learn from .
(12) The Associated Students Organization -LRB- ASO -RRB- is the official Student Government of the 23,000 students at Los Angeles Pierce College .
(13) It has been in existence since the mid-50 's and has three branches : Club Council , Senate and Student Court .
(14) Besides hosting the Brahmas ' football and women 's soccer teams , John Shepard Stadium -LRB- current capacity 5,500 -RRB- also has hosted many outdoor professional sporting events in San Fernando Valley history .
(15) From 1976 to 1979 , the San Fernando Valley 's first professional sports team , the Los Angeles Skyhawks of the American Soccer League , played their home games at the Pierce college stadium .
(16) The Los Angeles Express of the USFL played their last home game here on June 15 , 1985 .
(17) The stadium was expanded to 16,000-person capacity for the game .
(18) Shepard Stadium hosts Nuts for Mutts , an annual dog show and pet fair that raises funds for the New Leash on Life Animal Rescue .
(19) The stadium is also the former home stadium of the San Fernando Valley Quakes men 's soccer team , which competed in the USL Premier Development League .

(22) Center for the Sciences and a 50000 sqft .

(27) It features one of the finest collections of drought-resistant plants in the region .
(28) Pierce College prides itself as an environmentally-forward institution , with a 191-kilowatt solar generation system that has 1,274 photovoltaic panels and a 360-kilowatt , natural gas co-generation system .
(29) This project is the largest of its kind to be undertaken by a U.S. community college , yielding around 4.4 million kilowatt-hours of electricity a year and reducing Carbon dioxide emissions by more than 1,500 tons over its operating lifetime .
(30) The college also has a water retention pond beneath its soccer field , collecting run-off from the adjacent parking lot .

(33) The campus is home to `` Old Trapper 's Lodge , '' California Historical Landmark No. 939 , an outsider art environment that pays homage to the pioneer upbringing of its creator , John Ehn .
(34) It represents the life work of John Ehn -LRB- 1897 -- 1981 -RRB- , a self-taught artist who wished to pass on a sense of the Old West , derived from personal experiences , myths , and tall tales .
(35) From 1951 to 1981 , using his family as models , and incorporating memorabilia , the ` Old Trapper ' followed his dreams and visions to create the Lodge and its ` Boot Hill ' .
(36) The artwork was moved from the original site in Sun Valley , CA , and relocated to the college .
