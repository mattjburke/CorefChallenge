(1)Anatole France was a French poet , journalist , and novelist .
(2) He was born in Paris , and died in Saint-Cyr-sur-Loire .
(3) He was a successful novelist , with several best-sellers .
(4) Ironic and skeptical , he was considered in his day the ideal French man of letters .

(6) France is also widely believed to be the model for narrator Marcel 's literary idol Bergotte in Marcel Proust 's In Search of Lost Time .
(7) The son of a bookseller , France spent most of his life around books .
(8) France was a bibliophile .
(9) His father 's bookstore , called the Librairie France , specialized in books and papers on the French Revolution and was frequented by many notable writers and scholars of the day .
(10) Anatole France studied at the Collège Stanislas , a private Catholic school , and after graduation he helped his father by working in his bookstore .
(11) After several years he secured the position of cataloguer at Bacheline-Deflorenne and at Lemerre .
(12) In 1876 he was appointed librarian for the French Senate .
(13) Anatole France began his career as a poet and a journalist .

(15) In 1875 , he sat on the committee which was in charge of the third Parnasse Contemporain compilation .

(17) He became famous with the novel Le Crime de Sylvestre Bonnard -LRB- 1881 -RRB- .
(18) Its protagonist , skeptical old scholar Sylvester Bonnard , embodied France 's own personality .
(19) The novel was praised for its elegant prose and won him a prize from the Académie française .
