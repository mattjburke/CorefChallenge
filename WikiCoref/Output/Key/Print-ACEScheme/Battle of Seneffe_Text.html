(1)The Battle of Seneffe was fought on 11 August 1674 between French army under the command of Louis II de Bourbon , Prince de Condé and the Dutch-German-Spanish army under William III of Orange .
(2) During the Franco-Dutch war , William III commanded a Dutch-German-Spanish army through the southern Netherlands into the territory of Northern France .
(3) France defended this area with an army under Louis II de Bourbon , Prince de Condé .
(4) For five weeks the two armies manoeuvred without getting into combat with each other .
(5) On the 10th of August , William III decided to head for Paris in order to force the enemy into fighting .
(6) Condé sent a detachment of about 500 horsemen to keep the Dutch vanguard busy near the village of Seneffe , blocking the advance of William .
(7) In the meantime , Condé tried to surround the 60,000 allied troops with the 45,000 men at his disposal .
(8) The horsemen managed to keep the Dutch vanguard busy , but the envelopment of the main allied force failed .
(9) After ten hours Condé had 8,000 dead or wounded and William - 11,000 .
(10) Both armies retreated from the battlefield and although the battle was indecisive ; both sides claimed victory .
