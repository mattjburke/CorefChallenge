(1)Eldridge Pope was a traditional brewery situated in Dorchester , Dorset in England .
(2) The brewery opened in 1881 , and was run by a limited company - Eldridge Pope Limited - following the incorporation of that company on 2 March 1898 .
(3) Following a series of unfortunate business decisions , the brewery closed in July 2003 .
(4) The company 's best-known brands are now brewed under licence by Dorset and Devon-based micro-breweries .
(5) The site of its former brewery is currently being redeveloped under the name of Brewery Square .
(6) The origins of Eldridge Pope can be traced back to 1837 , when hotel landlord Charles Eldridge and his wife Sarah bought the Green Dragon Brewery in Dorchester .
(7) Together they turned the Brewery into a thriving small business , and following Charles 's death in 1846 , Sarah continued to run it in partnership with local brewer Samuel Mason , under the trading name ` Eldridge , Mason & Co ' .
(8) Upon his retirement in 1870 , Mason sold his stake in the Brewery to Edwin Pope and his younger sibling Alfred Pope .
(9) Sarah Eldridge 's son-in-law John Tizard inherited her share of the business , and when he died in 1871 the Popes assumed full control .
(10) By 1879 the company had outgrown its old premises , and the Pope brothers wanted to build a large new brewery to satisfy their expansion plans for the company .
(11) They bought up four acres of land next to the railway line , and commissioned distinguished local architect W.R Crickmay to design the new structure .
(12) The new brewery was officially opened in 1881 , and very quickly became the biggest employer in Dorchester .
(13) Sixteen years later the Pope brothers floated the business , forming a new limited company under the name ` Eldridge Pope & Co. Limited ' .
(14) The following decades saw the next generation of the Pope family take over the running of the company .
(15) In 1921 Clement Pope , son of Alfred , created the Huntsman trademark , which became one of the most recognizable and popular brands associated with the company .
(16) In 1922 a huge fire ripped through the brewery , damaging much of the brewhouse and several other buildings .
(17) It was not until 1925 that the rebuilding work was completed and the brewery was able to produce beer again .
(18) Denis Edwin Holliday was head brewer at Eldridge Pope throughout the 1960s and 70s .
(19) This era was associated with the production of popular real ales such as Royal Oak and Thomas Hardy 's Ale .
(20) Holliday also had an entry in the Guinness Book of Records , 1978 edition , for brewing the strongest commercially brewed beer .
(21) Following the brewery 's closure in 2003 , the company -LRB- latterly known as Eldridge Pope Inns Limited or EP Limited -RRB- continued to operate for a few years as a pub , bar and hotel business , running the Que Pasa chain of tapas bars , Room At the Inn hotels and H. J. Wellfeds pubs .
(22) In January/February 2007 , the assets of Eldridge Pope Inns Limited were sold to Marstons plc and the original quartet of Que Pasa properties sold off .
(23) Eldridge Pope Inns Limited was dissolved by voluntary strike-off on 16 February 2010 .
