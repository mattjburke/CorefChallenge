(1)Environmental science is a multidisciplinary academic field that integrates physical and biological sciences , -LRB- including but not limited to ecology , physics , chemistry , biology , soil science , geology , atmospheric science and geography -RRB- to the study of the environment , and the solution of environmental problems .
(2) Environmental science provides an integrated , quantitative , and interdisciplinary approach to the study of environmental systems .
(3) Related areas of study include environmental studies and environmental engineering .
(4) Environmental studies incorporates more of the social sciences for understanding human relationships , perceptions and policies towards the environment .
(5) Environmental engineering focuses on design and technology for improving environmental quality in every aspect .
(6) Environmental scientists work on subjects like the understanding of earth processes , evaluating alternative energy systems , pollution control and mitigation , natural resource management , and the effects of global climate change .
(7) Environmental issues almost always include an interaction of physical , chemical , and biological processes .
(8) Environmental scientists bring a systems approach to the analysis of environmental problems .
(9) Key elements of an effective environmental scientist include the ability to relate space , and time relationships as well as quantitative analysis .

(12) In common usage , `` environmental science '' and `` ecology '' are often used interchangeably , but technically , ecology refers only to the study of organisms and their interactions with each other and their environment .

(17) Atmospheric sciences focuses on the Earth 's atmosphere , with an emphasis upon its interrelation to other systems .
(18) Atmospheric sciences can include studies of meteorology , greenhouse gas phenomena , atmospheric dispersion modeling of airborne contaminants , sound propagation phenomena related to noise pollution , and even light pollution .
(19) Taking the example of the global warming phenomena , physicists create computer models of atmospheric circulation and infra-red radiation transmission , chemists examine the inventory of atmospheric chemicals and their reactions , biologists analyze the plant and animal contributions to carbon dioxide fluxes , and specialists such as meteorologists and oceanographers add additional breadth in understanding the atmospheric dynamics .
(20) Ecology is the study of the interactions between organisms and their environment .
(21) Ecologists might investigate the relationship between a population of organisms and some physical characteristic of their environment , such as concentration of a chemical ; or they might investigate the interaction between two populations of different organisms through some symbiotic or competitive relationship .
(22) For example , an interdisciplinary analysis of an ecological system which is being impacted by one or more stressors might include several related environmental science fields .
(23) In an estuarine setting where a proposed industrial development could impact certain species by water and air pollution , biologists would describe the flora and fauna , chemists would analyze the transport of water pollutants to the marsh , physicists would calculate air pollution emissions and geologists would assist in understanding the marsh soils and bay muds .
(24) Environmental chemistry is the study of chemical alterations in the environment .
(25) Principal areas of study include soil contamination and water pollution .
(26) The topics of analysis include chemical degradation in the environment , multi-phase transport of chemicals -LRB- for example , evaporation of a solvent containing lake to yield solvent as an air pollutant -RRB- , and chemical effects upon biota .
(27) As an example study , consider the case of a leaking solvent tank which has entered the habitat soil of an endangered species of amphibian .
(28) As a method to resolve or understand the extent of soil contamination and subsurface transport of solvent , a computer model would be implemented .
(29) Chemists would then characterize the molecular bonding of the solvent to the specific soil type , and biologists would study the impacts upon soil arthropods , plants , and ultimately pond-dwelling organisms that are the food of the endangered amphibian .
(30) Geosciences include environmental geology , environmental soil science , volcanic phenomena and evolution of the Earth 's crust .
(31) In some classification systems this can also include hydrology , including oceanography .
(32) As an example study of soils erosion , calculations would be made of surface runoff by soil scientists .
(33) Fluvial geomorphologists would assist in examining sediment transport in overland flow .
(34) Physicists would contribute by assessing the changes in light transmission in the receiving waters .
(35) Biologists would analyze subsequent impacts to aquatic flora and fauna from increases in water turbidity .
