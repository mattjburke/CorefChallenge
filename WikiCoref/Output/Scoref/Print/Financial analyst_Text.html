(1)A financial analyst , securities analyst , research analyst , equity analyst , or investment analyst is a person who performs financial analysis for external or internal clients as a core part of the job .
(2) Writing reports or notes expressing opinions is always a part of `` sell-side '' -LRB- brokerage -RRB- analyst job and is often not required for `` buy-side '' -LRB- investment firms -RRB- analysts .
(3) Traditionally , analysts use fundamental analysis principles but technical chart analysis and tactical evaluation of the market environment are also routine .
(4) Often at the end of the assessment of analyzed securities , an analyst would provide a rating recommending an investment action , e.g. to buy , sell , or hold the security .
(5) The analysts obtain information by studying public records and filings by the company , as well as by participating in public conference calls where they can ask direct questions to the management .
(6) Additional information can be also received in small group or one-on-one meetings with senior members of management teams .
(7) However in many markets such information gathering became difficult and potentially illegal due to legislative changes brought upon by corporate scandals in the early 2000s .
(8) One example is Regulation FD -LRB- Fair Disclosure -RRB- in the United States .
(9) Many other developed countries also adopted similar rules .
(10) Financial analysts are often employed by mutual and pension funds , hedge funds , securities firms , banks , investment banks , insurance companies , and other businesses , helping these companies or their clients make investment decisions .
(11) Financial analysts employed in commercial lending perform `` balance sheet analysis , '' examining the audited financial statements and corollary data in order to assess lending risks .
(12) In a stock brokerage house or in an investment bank , they read company financial statements and analyze commodity prices , sales , costs , expenses , and tax rates in order to determine a company 's value and project future earnings .
(13) In any of these various institutions , the analyst often meets with company officials to gain a better insight into a company 's prospects and to determine the company 's managerial effectiveness .
(14) Usually , financial analysts study an entire industry , assessing current trends in business practices , products , and industry competition .
(15) They must keep abreast of new regulations or policies that may affect the industry , as well as monitor the economy to determine its effect on earnings .
(16) Financial analysts use spreadsheet and statistical software packages to analyze financial data , spot trends , and develop forecasts ; see Financial modeling .
(17) On the basis of their results , they write reports and make presentations , usually making recommendations to buy or sell a particular investment or security .
(18) Senior analysts may actually make the decision to buy or sell for the company or client if they are the ones responsible for managing the assets .
(19) Other analysts use the data to measure the financial risks associated with making a particular investment decision .
(20) Financial analysts in investment banking departments of securities or banking firms often work in teams , analyzing the future prospects of companies that want to sell shares to the public for the first time .
(21) They also ensure that the forms and written materials necessary for compliance with Securities and Exchange Commission regulations are accurate and complete .
(22) They may make presentations to prospective investors about the merits of investing in the new company .
(23) Financial analysts also work in mergers and acquisitions departments , preparing analyses on the costs and benefits of a proposed merger or takeover .
(24) There are buy-side analysts and sell-side analysts .
(25) Some financial analysts collect industry data -LRB- mainly balance sheet , income statement and capital adequacy in banking sector -RRB- , merger and acquisition history and financial news for their clients .
(26) They normally standardize the different companies data to look uniform and facilitate their clients to do peer analysis .
(27) Their main objective is to enable their clients to make better decisions about the investment across different regions .
(28) They also provide the abundance of financial ratios calculated from the data that they gather from the financial statements that help clients to read the bottom line of the company .
(29) Many people mix up this with the data entry job but actually its far away from just data entry .
(30) Some financial analysts , called ratings analysts -LRB- who are often employees of ratings agencies -RRB- , evaluate the ability of companies or governments that issue bonds to repay their debt .
(31) On the basis of their evaluation , a management team assigns a rating to a company 's or government 's bonds .
(32) Other financial analysts perform budget , cost , and credit analysis as part of their responsibilities .
(33) At an increasingly large number of firms it is preferred that analysts earn the Chartered Financial Analyst -LRB- CFA -RRB- designation .
(34) There are also many regulatory requirements .
(35) For example , in the United States , sell-side or Wall Street research analysts must register with the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority -LRB- FINRA -RRB- .
(36) In addition to passing the General Securities Representative Exam , candidates must pass the Research Analyst Examination -LRB- series 86/series87 -RRB- in order to publish research for the purpose of selling or promoting publicly traded securities .
(37) Analyst recommendations on stocks owned by firms employing them may be seen as potentially biased .
(38) The research department sometimes does n't have the ability to bring in enough money to be a self-sustaining research company .
(39) The research analysts department is therefore sometimes part of the marketing department of an investment bank , brokerage , or investment advisory firm .
(40) Since 2002 there has been extra effort to overcome perceived conflicts of interest between the investment part of the firm and the public and client research part of the firm -LRB- see accounting scandals -RRB- .
(41) For example , research firms are sometimes separated into two categories , brokerage and independent .
(42) Independent researchers are not part of an investment firm and so do n't have the same incentive to issue overly favorable views on companies .
(43) But this might not be sufficient to avoid all conflicts of interest .
(44) Debate still exists about the way sell-side analysts are paid .
(45) Usually brokerage fees pay for their research .
(46) But this creates a temptation for analysts to act as stock sellers and to lure investors into `` overtrading '' .
(47) Some consider that it would be sounder if investors had to pay financial research separately and directly to fully independent research firms .
